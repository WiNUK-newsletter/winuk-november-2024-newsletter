Cerebral Palsy Awareness Day

This day is dedicated to raising awareness about cerebral palsy (CP), a group of neurological disorders that primarily affect movement, coordination, and posture, often due to brain damage occurring before, during, or shortly after birth.
CP is the most common motor disability in childhood, and it affects each individual differently.
The severity of the symptoms can range from mild, where a person may have slight motor difficulties, to severe, where a person may need assistance with most daily tasks.

Mental Health Awareness Day

Observed annually on October 10, Mental Health Awareness Day is dedicated to raising awareness about mental health issues, promoting mental well-being, and advocating for better mental health services and resources worldwide. It is also known as World Mental Health Day and is led by the World Health Organization (WHO) and the World Federation for Mental Health (WFMH). We also covered gender differences in mental health, influenced by both social and biological factors.
